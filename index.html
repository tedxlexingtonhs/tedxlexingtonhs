What is TEDx?

In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TED has created a program called TEDx. TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. Our event is called TEDxLexingtonHS, where x = independently organized TED event. At our TEDxLexingtonHS event, TED Talks video and live speakers will combine to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events, including ours, are self-organized.

ABOUT

About TEDx, x = independently organized event

In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. At a TEDx event, TED Talks video and live speakers combine to spark deep discussion and connection. These local, self-organized events are branded TEDx, where x = independently organized TED event. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events are self-organized. (Subject to certain rules and regulations.)

About TED

TED is a nonprofit organization devoted to Ideas Worth Spreading. Started as a four-day conference in California 30 years ago, TED has grown to support its mission with multiple initiatives. The two annual TED Conferences invite the world's leading thinkers and doers to speak for 18 minutes or less. Many of these talks are then made available, free, at TED.com. TED speakers have included Bill Gates, Jane Goodall, Elizabeth Gilbert, Sir Richard Branson, Nandan Nilekani, Philippe Starck, Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala, Sal Khan and Daniel Kahneman.

The annual TED Conference takes place each spring in Vancouver, British Columbia. TED's media initiatives include TED.com, where new TED Talks are posted daily; TED Translators, which provides subtitles and interactive transcripts as well as translations from volunteers worldwide; the educational initiative TED-Ed. TED has established The Audacious Project that takes a collaborative approach to funding ideas with the potential to create change at thrilling scale; TEDx, which supports individuals or groups in hosting local, self- organized TED-style events around the world, and the TED Fellows program, helping world-changing innovators from around the globe to amplify the impact of their remarkable projects and activities.

Follow TED on Twitter, or on Facebook.

Why we are Hosting a TEDx Event

TEDxLexingtonHS will connect the students and adults of Lexington as it will allow both of them to present the same platform as they will both get to speak and listen to others. The talks themselves will revolve around new concepts previously undiscussed, professional and personal experience, entrepreneurship, interdisciplinary connections as well as inspiring and interesting stories. These talks will empower both students, adults, and professionals with knowledge as well as become a part of forging a stronger community in Lexington. We invite you to select any topic with which you are familiar— our goal is for participants to share a diverse and engaging combination of skills, stories, and insights.

Our event is organized independently under a free license granted by TED. We will include a series of pre-selected live speakers, who will give 15-18 minute presentations.

SPEAKERS

Harvy Simkovits

Harvy Simkovits is a former engineer, business owner, management consultant, executive coach, and SOB (son of the boss). Since 2005, he has been writing and publishing stories about his finagling entrepreneurial father and tumultuous immigrant family. Just Lassen to Me! is his full-length memoir that was published in 2017 and became an Amazon Bestseller. A second volume was published in 2018, and a third will be released in 2019. He is a local Lexington resident who resides with his wife, two kids, and two cats.

Gene Beresin

Gene Beresin, MD, MA went to Princeton University where he majored in music. He attended the University of Pennsylvania Medical School for his MD and also received a Masters in Philosophy. He became a child and adolescent psychiatrist, and has taught medical students, residents and practicing physicians for 36 years. Dr. Beresin is Executive Director of the Clay Center for Young Healthy Minds, a free, accessible, online educational resource dedicated to promoting and supporting the mental, emotional, and behavioral well-being of children, teens, and young adults. He is a Professor of Psychiatry at Harvard Medical School. His interests include fostering healthy patient-doctor relationships, the positive development of youth and families, and destigmatizing mental illness. He is an active musician, performing and writing soundtracks for the Clay Center podcasts and videos.

Tammy Darling

Ms. Darling's two decades of innovative classroom teaching and diverse consulting work coalesced into a strengths-based positive education project to cultivate character in schools. Ms. Darling founded the Mighty Project in August of 2017, Mighty stands for Mentorship Introduces Great Habits ToYouth. She is an educator at Lexington High School and teaches Advanced Placement Psychology, Child Development and Race, Gender, and Human Behavior. She challenges her students to think about the diversity of human thought and behavior through reflective dialogue, driven by content, about evolving mind/body research findings that are crucial to healthy habits of mind.

Outside of the classroom, Ms. Darling is a lead educator and collaborator with Professor Danielle Allen at the Democratic Knowledge Project at Harvard University. She is currently collaborating with Professor Hardin Coleman at Boston University Wheelock College of Education and Human Development to investigate the efficacy of a developmental guidance activity to impact cognitive mindsets in 5th graders.

Pratibha Shah

Pratibha Shah, BAMS, MD-Ayurveda, MPH, is a leading Ayurevda Expert in the United States. Her pioneering initiatives in the field of Ayurveda, have brought her to attention at the White House, the Department of Health and Human Services, as well as the Consulate General of India, NYC. For her work, she was also nominated in the Top 20 Women of the Year, for the year 2014. She is a senior faculty and Ayurveda Domain Expert for Kerala Ayurveda Academy and CEO of My Ayurved LLC. She is the Founder, President of two non-profits - Council for Ayurveda Research and Wholistic Health Alliance. She currently consults out at her home office in Danvers and at Well Life Medical, a state of the art Integrative Medicine clinic in Massachusetts, USA. Before moving to the US, Shah was a Chief Medical Officer with the AYUSH ministry in India.

Nathan Ramesh

Nathan Ramesh is currently a senior at Lexington High School. From a young age, Nathan’s fascination with mathematics and passion for the physical sciences have driven him towards academic success. An ever-present competitor at school and regional math competitions alike, Nathan has accumulated a multitude of competitive scholastic awards over the years. Nathan is a well-known figure on the high school campus, contributing to the LHS culture with his infectious sense of humor and bold displays of “male fashion”. Some of Nathan’s other noteworthy achievements include contributions to several professional scientific publications, an active role in Lexington’s National Honor Society chapter, a longtime pursuit of the clarinet, and a lengthy commitment to peer support. Nathan’s impressive accomplishments have not gone unnoticed—following an early decision acceptance, Nathan will continue his education at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in the fall of 2019.

Joe Rancatore

Joe Rancatore grew up in Staten Island, New Jersey, and Saint Louis, Missouri. He moved to Boston in 1981, working with his brother at Toscanini's through 1985. He first opened Rancatore's ice cream in 1985. Following that, he opened Lexington's Ranctore's in 2004, and Newtonville Rancatore's in 2014. He married in 1987 and moved to Lexington in 1996, raising two sons who graduated from the Lexington High School in 2007 and 2009.

How to Register as a Speaker

To become a speaker, please submit a three to six minute video about your topic and your background no later than January 3, 2019 in this form. Our curating committee will be selecting the speakers list from these videos. We will email you if you are selected or if any materials are missing. If you need clarifications or have questions, email Alex Mingolla, Ishaan Khurana and Srinivasan Sathiamurthy at tedxlex@gmail.com
